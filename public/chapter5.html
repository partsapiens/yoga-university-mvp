5.1 Eight Limbs of Yoga

Outlined by Sage Patanjali, the eight limbs provide a map for holistic practice and spiritual growth:

  1. Yama — ethical disciplines
  2. Niyama — personal observances
  3. Asana — physical posture
  4. Pranayama — breath regulation
  5. Pratyahara — withdrawal of senses
  6. Dharana — concentration
  7. Dhyana — meditation
  8. Samadhi — integration / bliss

5.2 Yamas — Ethical Restraints

  • Ahimsa — non‑harming in thought, speech, action.
  • Satya — truthfulness and authenticity.
  • Asteya — non‑stealing; contentment with what is.
  • Brahmacharya — wise use of energy; moderation.
  • Aparigraha — non‑grasping; simplicity and generosity.

5.3 Niyamas — Observances

  • Shaucha — purity and clarity of body and mind.
  • Santosha — contentment and gratitude.
  • Tapas — discipline, heat, transformative effort.
  • Svadhyaya — self‑study and reflection.
  • Ishvara Pranidhana — surrender to the divine or highest self.

5.4 Yoga Sutras — Key Concepts

Patanjali's Yoga Sutras form the philosophical backbone of modern yoga. Central teachings include:

  • Chitta Vritti Nirodha — yoga as the quieting of mental fluctuations.
  • Abhyasa & Vairagya — sustained practice and non‑attachment.
  • Kleshas — root causes of suffering: ignorance, egoism, attachment, aversion, fear.
  • Kriya Yoga — the yoga of action: tapas, svadhyaya, Ishvara pranidhana.

5.5 Yogic Lifestyle & Modern Practice

Applying yoga philosophy off the mat creates coherence between practice and daily life:

  • Adopt balanced nutrition and mindful consumption.
  • Align work and relationships with the yamas and niyamas.
  • Integrate meditation and pranayama into daily routine.
  • Engage in seva (selfless service) and community care.

Yoga is a lifelong, evolving practice — both inner discipline and outward compassion.
