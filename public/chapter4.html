4.1 Principles of Assisting

  • Consent & choice: verbal opt‑in and clear non‑touch options every class.
  • See first, then say, then show: verbal → directional → demo → light touch when needed.
  • Stability before depth: foundation → spine → joints → refinements.
  • Neutral, supportive contact: broad, steady hands; avoid pushing joints into range.
  • Student‑directed depth: offer actions the student can create rather than imposing shape.

4.17 Props — Types & Uses

  • Blocks: floor rises to meet hands; stability in standing/folding and balance shapes.
  • Straps: extend reach; shoulder opening; bind rehearsal without spinal collapse.
  • Wall: alignment reference; load sharing for balance/inversions.
  • Bolsters/blankets: support joints; restorative comfort; subtle angle changes.

Props make poses truer, not easier—prioritize alignment and nervous‑system safety.

4.19 Safety & Contraindications

What is a contraindication?

A factor that advises caution or modification. Examples: acute injury, uncontrolled hypertension, recent surgery, pregnancy considerations, vertigo.

Teacher checklist

  • Offer at least one non‑weight‑bearing option for wrists/shoulders in vinyasa.
  • Keep neck neutral in folds and backbends; avoid end‑range loading.
  • Teach exit strategies for balances and deep hip shapes.
  • Invite self‑pacing, hydration, and rest at any time.
